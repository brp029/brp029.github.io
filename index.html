Surging Opportunity

Remote work opportunities surged in the U.S. during the Covid-19 pandemic. And while the rate of remote work has tapered off since its peak in the late spring of 2020, it remains well above pre-pandemic levels.

Remote Work by Demographics

In order to understand whether or not remote work is equitable, let's look at the breakdown of remote workers by different demographics.

Remote Equity

Despite the overall rise in workplace flexibility, not every U.S. worker was equally able to participate in the remote work boon.

While there are more men in the workforce, a higher percentage of female workers worked from home during the pandemic.

Data Sources

The data for this project was collected by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

The questions were incorporated into the Current Population Survey to "help gauge the effects of the coronavirus (COVID-19) pandemic on the labor market" and were collected monthly from May 2020 through September 2022.

The complete data set can be viewed on the BLS site.
